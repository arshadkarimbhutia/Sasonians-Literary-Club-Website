Robotics Symposium by SMIT 2024

Event Report

Tuesday, April 9 2024

On April 9th, 2024, St. Augustine School’s DJ Hall was abuzz with innovation as it hosted a robotics symposium for the senior science students of classes 11 and 12. The guest speakers were Dr. Dipanjan Bhattacharjee and Rabin Dahal. It was to be noted that Mr. Sanjay Verma had been in touch with them since a month and coordinated with them for a proper execution of this event.

After the operations and theories, the question session thus followed, to which the students were full of curiosity and asked several questions about the operation of the drone. Major questions consisted of drone stabilization, the role of Rigid Body Mechanics (RBM) in motor operation, and the philosophical query of whether robots could surpass humans. To which, the presenters offered a thought-provoking perspective: “Creation cannot be greater than the creator.” Post-discussion, the students were asked to allot themselves into a collaborative team of 5-10 members for the practical session.

They were all given arduino boards, chassis and some hardware for a basic wheel-bot. This board is essentially a small computer in which one uploads the code and program for the mechanics and technicalities of the robot to make it work. He also focused on briefing students about the coding aspect of the bot's working. Dr. Dipanjan then attempted an experiment with his drone to showcase that if programmed not properly in an enclosed environment a misstep in switching from altitude hold mode to auto pilot, will cause failure in the drone.

With this experiment he pointed out that failures happen and we should learn from our mistakes. A team triumphed in assembling an Arduino Uno-based mobile vehicle, complete with sensors, which operated flawlessly. Post-lunch, the students were tasked with programming their creations. With sensor integration, they successfully achieved in moving their vehicles. Mr. Dahal supervised this session, ensuring the functionality of each prototype. After being done with all the models the guests finally concluded the event by performing a drone show and elaborating the process behind a successful flight operation.

The school prefect, Aayan Yogesh Khati, articulated the collective gratitude of the students, thanking the guests for their invaluable insights. In a gesture of appreciation, Father George presented the guests with tokens of appreciation, celebrating their commitment to elucidating the complexities of robotics to the students.
